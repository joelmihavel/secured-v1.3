3. Rental Income Protection Plan – Coverage Details

The Rental Income Protection Plan provides financial protection against two critical rental income risks. This guarantee is financially backed by Slaash Technologies Private Limited (Flent Secured) through our institutional risk management partnerships.

3.1 What is Covered

Un-occupancy Guarantee

If your property remains vacant for 30 days after a tenant moves out and Flent Secured is unable to find a replacement tenant, you will receive coverage of up to one month's rent (maximum ₹1,50,000).

Conditions for Un-occupancy coverage:

  • The departing tenant must have served a minimum 30-day notice period through the Flent Secured platform
  • The property must remain listed and actively available on Flent Secured during the vacant period
  • You must accept a new tenant who meets the agreed rental terms (including 5-10% renewal escalation if applicable)

Tenant Abandonment Coverage

If a tenant abandons the property and it remains vacant beyond the security deposit adjustment period (minimum 60 days), you will receive coverage of up to one month's rent (maximum ₹1,50,000).

Conditions for Abandonment coverage:

  • Tenant abandonment must be verified through Flent Secured's verification process
  • You must accept a new tenant who meets the agreed rental terms (including 5-10% renewal escalation if applicable)

3.3 Coverage Activation Requirements

Your protection activates when all five conditions are met:

  1. Three successful payments: Tenant completes minimum 3 rent payments through Flent Secured
  2. 90-day waiting period: Coverage begins 90 days from enrollment date
  3. Continuous platform usage: All subsequent rent payments processed through Flent Secured
  4. Verified tenant: Tenant completes identity and address verification
  5. Active enrollment: Property remains listed on Flent Secured throughout coverage period
Practical Example:
Enrollment date: March 1, 2026
First payment: March 5, 2026
Third payment: May 5, 2026
Coverage active: June 1, 2026 (90 days post-enrollment + 3 payments complete)

4. Payment Processing and Settlement

4.1 How Payments Work

  • Your tenant initiates rent payment through Flent Secured's platform using their preferred payment method
  • Payment is processed through our authorized RBI-regulated payment gateway partners
  • Rent is settled directly to your registered bank account (less any applicable platform fees, if introduced)
  • Settlement typically occurs within 1-2 business days of successful payment processing

Payment Flow: Tenant → Payment Gateway Partner → Your Bank Account
Flent Secured coordinates this process as your rental management service provider.

4.2 Payment Records and Documentation

Flent Secured maintains auditable records of all rent transactions, which:

  • Provide verifiable proof of consistent rental income
  • Are accessible 24/7 through your secure landlord dashboard
  • Can be downloaded for income verification, tax compliance, or financial applications
  • Include transaction IDs, timestamps, and payment confirmation from our gateway partners

4.3 Your Bank Account

You must provide and maintain accurate bank account details. Changes to your bank account must be updated promptly through the platform. Flent Secured is not responsible for payment delays or failures due to incorrect bank details.

9.2 Mandatory Verification for Claim Disbursement

Before any claim payout is processed, the following verifications must be completed:

  • Landlord KYC: Valid government-issued identity proof (PAN + Aadhaar-based e-KYC), bank account verification linked to the registered name, and contact verification.
  • Property Ownership Verification: Proof of ownership (registered sale deed, property tax receipt, or equivalent document), confirmation that the property address matches records, and any additional documentation reasonably requested to establish lawful ownership.
  • Tenant Verification: Confirmation that the tenant was onboarded through Flent Secured's verified onboarding process, tenancy agreement on record, and verification of the qualifying event (vacancy, abandonment) through platform records and supporting documentation.

Important

Incomplete KYC, unverified ownership, or unverified tenancy will result in claim processing delays or denial. We strongly recommend completing all verifications at the time of onboarding to avoid delays at the time of a claim.

9.4 Claim Approval Criteria

Claims are approved when:

  • Event is covered (un-occupancy or abandonment)
  • All activation requirements were met (3 payments, 90-day waiting period, active enrollment)
  • Landlord KYC, property ownership, and tenant verification are complete
  • Complete documentation provided
  • No exclusion conditions apply

9.5 Claim Denial Reasons

Common reasons for denial:

  • Coverage not yet active (waiting period or payment requirement not met)
  • Qualified replacement tenant rejected by landlord
  • Property was undergoing renovation during vacancy
  • Incomplete KYC, ownership verification, or tenant verification
  • Incomplete or missing documentation
  • Exclusion condition applies
